The Through-Line: Creating Better Systems

For 29 years, I've been building systems that help people work better. It started before I even got into tech—as a college warehouse manager, I was optimizing workflow and reducing bottlenecks. At Microsoft, I created the VINCE tool to make player data visible to developers. At every company since, I've identified gaps, created systems, and opened career opportunities for the people I work with.

It's always been people-centered. I hear the problems teams are facing, fill the gaps, and create systems that help people thrive. That's the through-line of my career.

From Political Science to Xbox: An Accidental Path to Tech

Between ages 10-14, I had one dream: make video games. Then I went to college and pivoted hard into political science. Not because I wanted to be in politics—I was fascinated by systems. How do projects move through processes? How do laws pass? How is society governed? I loved understanding how the pieces fit together.

The tech pivot happened by accident. My roommate was studying for a Microsoft networking certification, and I realized I understood the material better than he did. I could see how the processes and projects worked together. Around the same time, I hacked the college network to play multiplayer Doom (this was 1990-91, when that wasn't common).

After graduation, I worked at Aviation Supplies & Academics doing tech support and QA for flight simulator software. I realized my passion was software, not politics. So I enrolled at the University of Washington for computer science, met someone at Microsoft Games, and within six weeks, I had my dream job at the Microsoft Games Organization. That was 1999.

The AI Transition: From Systems Guy to AI Builder

I started working with AI about 2.5 years ago. According to ChatGPT's 2025 year-in-review report, I'm in the top 1% of users and top 3% for conversation volume.

AI clicked for me immediately because I'm a systems thinker. I've always seen connections others couldn't follow. AI gave me structure to create "straight lines" so others could see what I was seeing. It enabled Resilient Tomorrow, where AI helped me organize my thoughts into meaningful conversations.

I quickly mastered prompting, but I realized the real key is context—give AI the right context and it can help you find answers. Once I saw that AI could help me code, build prototypes, and create things I wanted to see in the world, there was no stopping it.

Now I'm not just using AI systems—I'm building them. I've created an AI Memory System for persistent context, set up local LLM infrastructure for privacy, and developed the AI-Augmented Process Design (AAPD) methodology for my clients.
